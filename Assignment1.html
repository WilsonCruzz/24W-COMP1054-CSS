About


Mariah Carey is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, actress, and entrepreneur. She was born on March 27, 1969, in Huntington, New York. Mariah Carey rose to fame in the early 1990s with her powerful vocals, five-octave vocal range, and innovative use of melisma. She is often referred to as the "Songbird Supreme" and the "Queen of Christmas" for her iconic holiday music. Carey's debut album, "Mariah Carey" (1990), included four number-one singles on the Billboard Hot 100 and established her as a prominent figure in the music industry. Throughout her career, she has achieved numerous chart-topping hits and has become one of the best-selling music artists of all time. In addition to her success in music, Mariah Carey has also ventured into acting. She made her debut in the film "Precious" (2009) and has appeared in other projects, including "The Butler" (2013) and "A Christmas Melody" (2015). Mariah Carey's influence extends beyond the entertainment industry, and she has been recognized for her philanthropy work and contributions to the music community. Her impact on pop and R&B music, along with her vocal prowess, has solidified her as an enduring and influential figure in the world of entertainment.

Album

"Merry Christmas" is the fourth studio album by Mariah Carey, released on November 1, 1994. The album is a holiday classic and features a mix of traditional Christmas songs and original compositions. "Merry Christmas" showcases Mariah Carey's exceptional vocal range and festive spirit. The album includes her iconic holiday anthem "All I Want for Christmas Is You," which has become a perennial favorite during the Christmas season. In addition to the upbeat and joyous tracks, Carey also delivers soulful renditions of classic Christmas carols, infusing them with her signature style. The album's timeless appeal has made it a holiday staple and a must-listen for fans of Christmas music.
